Proyan
The Proyan Solution

One integrated platform
that solves it all

Instead of 5+ apps, Proyan combines real-time traffic, multimodal planning, vehicle management, government services, and safety — all in one place.

AI Traffic + Route Planning

Problem: No live data → unpredictable delays, no alternate routes.

Solution: Real-time congestion from community & AI with automatic rerouting.

Multimodal & E-ticketing

Problem: Queue at counters, physical tickets, mode switching.

Solution: Plan bus + metro + ride in one flow; buy digital tickets and scan QR.

SOS & Safety Alerts

Problem: No emergency help in apps; 74% pedestrian accident rate.

Solution: One-tap SOS to 3 contacts, locate nearest police booth, share live location.

Integrated MFS Payments

Problem: Multiple wallets, cash for tolls, no unified transport payment.

Solution: Unified wallet for tickets, tolls, taxes — via bKash, Nagad, Rocket, Upay or card.

Vehicle Maintenance & Docs

Problem: Missed BRTA renewals, physical paperwork, no digital tools.

Solution: Track schedules, renewal reminders, store digital fitness certificates and road tax docs.

Proyan Community

Problem: No shared, real-time commuter intelligence in BD transport apps.

Solution: Users post jams, accidents, route changes — feeds directly into the AI traffic layer.

User Research

Four pain points
backed by real data

01

No Real-Time Multimodal Information

Commuters can't see live bus locations, metro schedules, or rickshaw availability in one place. 87.5% cited real-time updates as their top need.

02

Fragmented Payment Experience

Commuters switch between bKash, Nagad, and cash per journey leg. Despite 238M+ MFS accounts in Bangladesh, no transport app unifies these payments.

03

No Safety Infrastructure in Apps

74% of Dhaka road accidents involve pedestrians. No existing transport app offers SOS, police locator, or real-time safety alerts.

04

Manual Vehicle & Document Management

Private vehicle owners manage fitness certificates, road tax, and BRTA renewals entirely offline, with no digital reminders or submission tools.

Trade-offs

Balancing complexity & usability

Launch modes vs. UX simplicityStart with 4 core modes (Bus, MRT, Bike, Car). Rickshaw in V2 — no API exists yet.
Build map vs. use Google MapsGoogle Maps as base for accuracy + speed. Proyan adds community AI layer on top.
Own payment processor vs. MFS gatewaysUsers trust bKash, Nagad already. Integrating lowers friction and avoids payment licensing overhead.
BRTA in MVP vs. V2BRTA digitization timeline is uncertain — deferred until the API stabilizes.
Dhaka-only vs. multi-city launchDhaka has the most acute problem. Single-city focus ensures quality and community density at launch.

Risk Register

Anticipating challenges

High

API Reliability from Transport Providers

Pathao, DMTCL APIs may have downtime. Mitigation: graceful fallback to Google Maps + manual mode selection.

High

Informal Mode Coverage Gap

Rickshaws lack digital tracking. Mitigation: community reporting fills gap in V1; GPS integration roadmapped for V2.

Med

Community Cold Start Problem

Traffic community is useless without users. Mitigation: seed with Google Maps data; incentivize early reporters with wallet credits.

Med

Regulatory Shifts in Ride-sharing / MFS

Bangladesh ride-share laws evolving. Mitigation: design API abstraction layer to swap providers without core app changes.

Low

User Adoption vs. Pathao/Uber Habit

Strong incumbents. Mitigation: Proyan's value is multimodal + safety + docs — a category existing apps don't compete in.

PM Reflection

Key takeaways

01

Real data changes every decision

Dhaka's speed dropping from 21 to 7 km/h reframed the problem from "inconvenience" to "economic crisis." Numbers make every product decision sharper.

02

Constraints are design inputs

Rickshaws have no APIs. BRTA is still digitizing. Building around infrastructure constraints rather than against them was the key to a credible MVP.

03

Timing is a product feature

MFS adoption, metro rail launch, and BRTA digitization all happening in the same window creates a rare market moment.

04

Language unlocks honesty

Conducting interviews in Bangla surfaced nuances missed in English — like how commuters rely on Facebook groups for bus schedules.
